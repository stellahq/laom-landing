## (For those who missed a chapter!) What is LAOM?

LAOM is a living and welcoming place nestled in a wild valley in the south of Aveyron, where nature, creation, collective life, and events come together.

**It is:**

- A creative place that has already welcomed hundreds of people: artists, entrepreneurs, builders, and those seeking calm and renewal, from all walks of life.
- A 120 m² dance and practice room with large windows (built entirely through participatory builds).
- Berber tents and tipis for summer stays.
- A future 7-bedroom coliving for medium-term stays and artistic, entrepreneurial, and wellness residencies.
- A permaculture market garden to feed residents and guests.
- A future guinguette-style restaurant and coworking space.
- Healthy, ecological housing for year-round residents.
- And a magical river!

*A place to live differently, work differently, and connect differently.*

We're experimenting with eco-construction techniques here, including a huge load-bearing straw building (possibly the largest in the world!) that will house the future coliving, a common room, and permanent or semi-permanent homes (all already reserved!). For those who know the place, this building is called [**Grand Shambala**](/en/grand-shambala-v2/).

Today, LAOM is both: a build site, a place that already welcomes the public with events, retreats, and workshops, and a human adventure, with its momentum and its friction.

## Build update

Since our last newsletter, the build has moved forward a lot. On Grand Shambala in particular:

- **External earth-lime renders** were completed in late summer 2025—they look amazing, we love the result but it took a huge amount of time!
- **The first layer of internal earth plaster** has also been completed.
- Charly made a **corten steel LAOM sign** that was mounted on the wooden west gable.
- **Roof insulation** is done, with blown chopped straw in the loft: cubic metres of straw that brought their share of complexity—chopping a mass of straw, moving the chopped straw upstairs, then blowing it into the loft (we're talking hundreds of m² of loft!).
- **All 88 windows** have arrived and were installed in November!
- **The first internal partitions** are in place on the upper floor and the interior spaces are taking shape.

We've been busy!

## Where does the collective stand?

A **STECAL** (Secteur de Taille et de Capacité d'Accueil Limitée) is a planning instrument. In practice, it allows a project in a natural or agricultural zone, like LAOM, to obtain a specific authorisation to build, renovate, or develop buildings, while respecting strict environmental and landscape rules. It's a regulated derogation, granted only when:

- the project is coherent with the territory;
- its environmental impact is under control;
- and its usefulness is recognised locally.

**In short, we got our STECAL approved and it's huge!**

Charly has driven and steered this whole administrative and legal side. In recent days, a final push has moved this long-running dossier decisively forward.

We've found a model that respects our need for autonomy, our desire for freedom, and the momentum of community. We'll explain it all in more detail very soon (including in LAOM School)!

Originally, eight of us carried the impulse to create the ecolieu and acquire the place. We will soon be 18 on site.
