## How the Shala came to be

Le Shala — dance and practice hall — took shape over one summer, built almost entirely through participatory construction.
A space built by bodies in motion, hands at work, and collective energy.

The project was born when Eliott Meunier joined the adventure and Charly Aubert shared his vision of a room dedicated to practice. For Eliott, having a dance hall had long been a dream.
Around this shared momentum, we brought together Eliott's community for participatory construction, and the Shala came to life in a few months.

![Exterior view of the Shala, the building in natural materials in the Aveyron landscape, golden late-afternoon light, hills in the background](/images/laom/placeholder-shala-exterieur.webp)

## Plaster as art

In April 2024, working lime-sand plasters with pigments from the local earth, something shifted.

Hands in the material. Colour emerging from the mix. The gesture repeating, refining, finding its rhythm. It was no longer just construction — it was art.

That moment was a revelation. Ecological building and artistic creation are not separate. A well-made plaster holds the same beauty as a canvas. That is what we call the Fractal of Beauty: beauty at every scale, from the smallest detail to the whole.

![Detail of earth plasters in the Shala, close-up on texture, natural pigments, colour nuances. Hands working the material.](/images/laom/placeholder-shala-enduits-detail.webp)

## A space for transformation

Today Le Shala hosts movement workshops, dance sessions, and collective practice. It is here that LAOM's signature immersions take place — experiences where the body takes over from words and movement becomes the path of transformation.

![Interior of the empty Shala, generous volume, natural light through large openings, natural materials on the floor, view of the Grands Causses hills](/images/laom/placeholder-shala-interieur-vide.webp)

120 m² of floor, light, and space. No mirrors. No bars. Just a generous volume, living materials, and the view over the hills of the Grands Causses.

The room breathes. Earth-plaster walls regulate humidity naturally. The air is clean. Light comes in through large openings. The space invites you to move, dare, and let yourself be surprised.
